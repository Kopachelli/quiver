Privacy Policy

Last updated: 6 June 2026

Quiver does not collect, transmit, store, or share any personal data. It is a local Windows desktop app that runs entirely on your device.

What Quiver does

  • No telemetry, analytics, or tracking. No usage tracking, no analytics SDKs, no remote crash reporting.
  • No network connections. Quiver makes no internet or server connections — it doesn't "phone home," check for updates online, or send anything anywhere.
  • Local files only. It reads and edits the skill, MCP‑server, and plugin files your agents already keep in your home directory (e.g. %USERPROFILE%\.cursor, \.claude, \.codex…). These never leave your computer.
  • Settings & logs stay local. Settings live at %APPDATA%\Quiver\settings.json; a diagnostic note may be written to %APPDATA%\Quiver\crash.log on error. Neither is ever transmitted.
  • No accounts, no sign‑in, no cookies.

The only actions involving other programs are ones you explicitly trigger — "Reveal in Explorer", "Open in Default App", "Open in Cursor" — which launch local apps on your PC.

Website & downloads

The Quiver website is hosted on GitHub Pages and downloads come from GitHub Releases. When you visit those, GitHub may process standard request data (such as IP address and user agent) under GitHub's Privacy Statement. That is GitHub's processing, not Quiver's.
